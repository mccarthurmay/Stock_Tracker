================================================================================
PAPER-TEST PLAN — $100,000
Leveraged core (UPRO, crash-dodged) + value anchors (BRK.B / IWN) + cash
================================================================================

PURPOSE
  This is an EXPERIMENT with a control group, not a portfolio. Paper money's only
  honest use: find out whether the backtested behavior holds up live, and practice
  executing rules WITHOUT discretion. Success is NOT "made the most money" — it is
  clean execution, measured slippage, and sleeves behaving as backtested.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ALLOCATION  ($100,000)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Sleeve            Ticker    $          %     Managed how
  ---------------   -------   --------   ---   -----------------------------
  Leveraged core    UPRO      $50,000    50%   CRASH-DODGE (active, see rules)
  Value anchor 1    BRK.B     $20,000    20%   Plain buy & hold (DO NOT dodge)
  Value anchor 2    IWN       $20,000    20%   Plain buy & hold (DO NOT dodge)
  Cash / T-bills    BIL       $10,000    10%   Dry powder + yield; rebal buffer

  WHY THIS SHAPE (the rules learned from backtesting):
   - Crash-dodge ONLY the leveraged sleeve. Dodging the calm anchors (SPY/BRK/IWN)
     was tested and LOST in both bull and dot-com — they get whipsawed with no
     crash to avoid. Anchors are ballast; let them sit.
   - Anchors are VALUE / NON-TECH on purpose. The dot-com bear was a tech unwind;
     value (BRK, small-cap value) was THE hedge. Anchoring with the tech-heavy
     S&P is what leaves you underwater in that tail.
   - 20/20 BRK+IWN instead of 40% of one = diversified against being WRONG about
     any single anchor's crash behavior. Costs a hair of expected return for
     robustness.
   - NO VIXY / options insurance. Tested at 5% on every mix — it LOST money in
     BOTH the bull and the dot-com (premium bleed > payoff at that size, and the
     crash-dodge already provides the protection). The 10% cash is the hedge.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
UPRO SLEEVE — THE CI CRASH-DODGE   (check FRIDAY close, act MONDAY)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Parameters: 90-day lookback, 2.0-sigma band, 8 staggered steps.
    (2.0-sigma = the "95% CI" analysis.py already computes: ci = std * 2.
     The 8-step / 2.0-sigma region is the robust plateau from the sweep.
     The 1.5-sigma / 10-step = higher backtest $ is an OVERFIT spike — do NOT use.)

  Each Friday, from UPRO daily closes compute:
     90-day mean
     90-day std
     lower band = mean - 2 * std

  STATE = NORMAL (sleeve fully in UPRO):
     - UPRO closes BELOW the lower band
          -> SELL ALL UPRO to cash. Enter CRASH. Record the closing low.

  STATE = CRASH:
     - UPRO makes a NEW closing low
          -> buy back 1/8 of the sleeve (8 steps = average-down on the way down).
     - UPRO closes ABOVE its 90-day mean
          -> buy back to FULL, return to NORMAL.

  Step size: ~$50,000 / 8 = ~$6,250 per step.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ANCHORS + CASH
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  BRK.B, IWN: buy once, hold. Do not touch except the quarterly rebalance.
  BIL: source/sink for the UPRO crash-dodge and for quarterly rebalancing.
  QUARTERLY: rebalance all four sleeves back to 50 / 20 / 20 / 10.

BACKTEST CONTEXT  ($100,000, quarterly rebalanced)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  BULL (real 2016-2026, ~10.4 yrs):
     END ~$1,355,000   CAGR ~28.5%   maxDD ~40%

  DOT-COM stress (CONSTRUCTED: actual 2016->Feb2020, then the 2000-02 dot-com
  S&P path spliced in place of COVID; UPRO = decayed synthetic 3x; BRK & IWN
  modeled as the value/defensive winners they were in 2000-02):
     ends ~$299,000   trough ~$84,000   maxDD ~51%   — STAYS ABOVE the $100k start

  For comparison (same 50% UPRO-cd core, different anchor):
     40% BRK.B only ...... BULL ~$1,440,000  | DOT-COM ~$357,000
     40% small-value only  BULL ~$1,253,000  | DOT-COM ~$300,000
     40% SPY (tech-heavy)  BULL ~$1,441,000  | DOT-COM ~$88,000  (BELOW start!)

  THE DOLLAR-PATH REFRAME (why the big maxDD % is less scary than it sounds):
     The drawdown is measured from the PEAK after years of gains, NOT from $100k.
     In the dot-com stress this mix peaks ~$272k, troughs ~$84k, ends ~$299k.
     Worst dip below your STARTING $100k is only ~$16k — you give back GAINS, you
     do not lose most of your principal. (This is true BECAUSE the crash-dodge +
     value anchors hold up; a 50% UPRO buy-and-hold with an SPY anchor goes
     UNDERWATER for the decade.)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PRE-REGISTERED EXPECTATIONS  (so you can't rationalize later)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - In a calm/up market the leverage carries you; expect BIG numbers and a
    ~40% peak drawdown at some point. That 40% is NORMAL for this mix, not a
    failure. Do not panic-sell it — the plan ASSUMES you ride it.
  - The crash-dodge will mostly stay ~100% invested and only de-risk in genuinely
    down/volatile stretches (in backtest: 2018, 2020, 2022). If it's trading a
    lot in a calm market, something is mis-implemented.
  - The value anchors will LAG in a tech-led bull (that's their job — they pay
    off in the tech-unwind tail, not the melt-up). Lagging is expected.

  SUCCESS = (1) executed every rule with zero discretion,
            (2) measured real slippage vs the model,
            (3) sleeves behaved as backtested.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
REVIEW + DECISION RULES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Monthly: update log, one-paragraph summary.
  6-month review (must include some volatility to count): compare each sleeve and
  the total to the 100%-SPY control on SHARPE, DRAWDOWN, and DOLLARS.
     - Crash-dodge fills materially worse than prior-close
          -> the cost model was optimistic (a REAL finding; size it down).
     - Total doesn't clearly beat a simple 60/40 SPY/cash on a risk-adjusted basis
       after a volatile stretch -> the complexity isn't earning its keep.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE CAVEATS THAT MATTER MOST  (read before trusting any number above)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  1. REGIME LUCK. Every bull number ($1.3M+) is from ONE benign decade with no
     dot-com/2008-style choppy bear. The data CANNOT prove this survives the tail.
     The whole point of the value anchors + cash is to prepare for the tail the
     backtest can't see.

  2. THE DOT-COM NUMBERS ARE CONSTRUCTED, NOT REAL. Free data starts 2016, so
     2000-02 and 2008 are absent. The ~$299k dot-com result leans on BRK.B and
     small-cap value REPEATING their 2000-02 outperformance. That's true for a
     TECH-UNWIND bear (real history) but NOT a 2008-style everything-crash, where
     these anchors fall too. Treat the dot-com figures as "right direction,
     plausible magnitude," not a guarantee.

  3. WHIPSAW RISK. The crash-dodge HELPS one clean crash but BLEEDS through a
     prolonged choppy bear (sell the break, buy the bounce, repeat). It is not a
     magic shield; it is a tool that trades bull upside for one-crash protection.

  4. NONE OF THIS IS DSR-SIGNIFICANT. Across the whole research project, no
     strategy cleared the data-mining-adjusted significance bar. The drawdown
     REDUCTION is the robust part; the exact dollar outcomes are not repeatable
     on demand.

  5. PAPER FILLS ARE IDEALIZED — better than real, no emotional pressure. So
     underperformance on paper is damning; outperformance should be discounted.

  This is a structured LEARNING plan on paper money, not advice to deploy real
  capital. 50% in a 3x fund is aggressive; sizing leverage against tail risk is
  worth a fee-only advisor before any of this touches real dollars.
